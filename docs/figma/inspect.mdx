title: Inspect Styles
description: "Read any layer's LVGL style straight from Figma and copy it into your XML: colors, borders, radius, and size, with no export needed."
---

Sometimes you don't want to export a whole screen. You just want to know what one
layer looks like in LVGL and drop that style into a file you're already writing.
Inspect mode is for exactly that: pick a layer, read its [LVGL style](../syntax/styles), copy it.

It's a read-only view. It never writes files and never changes your design, and it works
entirely on your machine.

## Reading a layer's style

Open Inspect from the `</>` icon at the top of the plugin, beside the mode toggle. Then
select a single layer on the Figma canvas.

The plugin shows you everything it knows about that layer:

- **The LVGL style snippet**: the layer's look expressed as LVGL style attributes,
  syntax-highlighted so it's easy to read, covering its color, border, radius, padding,
  and size (the visual properties that layer would carry as a widget).
- **Layer details**: the layer's icon, type, dimensions, and node id, so you can confirm
  you're looking at the right thing.

If nothing is selected, you'll just see "Select a layer." Pick one node and the panel
fills in.

<Figure src="/_static/images/figma/inspect-mode.png" alt="Inspect mode showing a layer's LVGL style snippet" caption="Select one layer and Inspect shows its LVGL style, layer details, and a Copy button." />

## Copying the style

There's a **Copy** button on the panel. It copies the style attributes only, just the
part you'd paste into a `<style>` in your XML, not the surrounding tags.

Paste it into the widget you're hand-writing, and you're done. Read here, paste there,
with no files written to disk.

<Callout type="info">
  Want the plugin to write real files instead of copying snippets by hand? That's what
  [Export](./exporting) is for: it hands whole screens and components to the
  LVGL Pro Editor. Inspect is the lightweight, copy-one-layer alternative.
</Callout>
