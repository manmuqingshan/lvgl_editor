title: Exporting
description: Send your design tokens, components, screens, or whole prototype flows from Figma to LVGL Pro Editor with one click.
---

When your design is ready, **Export mode** is where you hand it off. Pick what you want to
send (your styles, your components, a few screens, or an entire flow) and the plugin
passes it to the LVGL Pro Editor, writing the XML files to your project.

<Figure
  src="/_static/images/figma/export-mode.png"
  alt="Export mode showing the Design Tokens and Screens sections"
  caption="Export mode groups every action into Design Tokens and Screens. Each row exports on its own."
/>

<Callout type="warn">
  You can't export until a **display size** is set. It's the one required setting. If you
  haven't, the plugin drops you into the **Project** view and flags the field. See
  [Project settings](./project-settings).
</Callout>

## The export actions

### Design Tokens

Your **tokens** are the saved colors, fonts, numbers, and text you reuse across a design,
these are your Figma styles.

<Cards>
  <Card icon={<Palette />} title="All Globals">
    Sends every color, font, number, and string in your file, along with your paint, text and
    effect. The editor turns them into one shared token file your whole
    project can reference.
  </Card>

  <Card icon={<Blocks />} title="All Components">
    Sends every [component](../syntax/components) and all of its variants. This one always ships your tokens too, so
    the shared token file stays in sync.
  </Card>
</Cards>

### Screens

A [**screen**](../syntax/screens) is one full view, a frame that becomes a screen in the running interface.

<Cards>
  <Card icon={<Monitor />} title="Selected Screens">
    Exports whatever you've picked on the canvas, **plus only the components and tokens they actually
    use**. You get a self-contained set of files with nothing extra.
  </Card>

  <Card icon={<Zap />} title="Flows">
    Exports a whole prototype flow at once: a group of screens wired together by your Figma
    prototype links. More on this below.
  </Card>
</Cards>

## Exporting a flow

If you've wired your screens together with Figma's prototype connections, the plugin can
follow those links and export a whole journey in one go: a login flow, an onboarding
sequence, a settings path.

The **Flows** row works a little differently from the others. Instead of exporting on
click, it opens a panel.

<Steps>

<Step>
**Open the panel.** Click the Flows row and it expands inline. The first time you open it,
the plugin traces your prototype links, and you'll see a brief *"Tracing prototype flows…"*
while it maps out how your screens connect.
</Step>

<Step>
**Pick a flow.** Each flow it finds shows up as its own row, with a name and a count of the
screens it contains. Any screens that aren't linked into a flow get gathered into a **Loose
Screens** group so nothing is left behind.
</Step>

<Step>
**Export it.** Every flow row has its own **Export** button, click it and it exports those screens as well as their related tokens and components.
</Step>

</Steps>

<Figure
  src="/_static/images/figma/export-flows.png"
  alt="The Flows panel expanded with detected flows"
  caption="The Flows panel lists every flow the plugin traced from your prototype links."
/>

<Callout type="info">
  A **flow** is a set of screens connected by your prototype links, the same word Figma
  uses. If the plugin says *"no flows detected,"* your screens probably aren't wired
  together yet; you can still send them with **Selected Screens**.
</Callout>

## After you export

Each row remembers what it did. Once an export finishes, it will provide a short summary *"128 design tokens ·
14 files · just now."* That relative time keeps itself current, so a glance tells you how
fresh each part of your project is.

Where do the files go? Straight into your LVGL project on disk: the tokens, components,
screens, and images all land in a tidy folder layout that the service manages for you.
