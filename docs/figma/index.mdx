title: Figma Flow

---

Design your interface in Figma the way you always have. **LVGL Flow for Figma** reads
your file (screens, components, colors, fonts, spacing) and turns it into [LVGL XML](../syntax/introduction)
files you can open, run, and refine in [LVGL Pro Editor](../editor).

You don't write any code to get started. You design; the plugin and the Editor do the
translation. And because a developer stays in control of the final structure, nothing you
hand off ever locks them in.

<Callout type="success">
  **LVGL Flow for Figma** workflow requires LVGL Pro Editor v2.0.0+ to work.
</Callout>

## How it fits together

There are two halves. The **plugin** lives in Figma and reads your design. **The service**
lives inside LVGL Pro Editor and writes the LVGL files to your project. They talk to each
other on your own machine.
